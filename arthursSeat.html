Arthur's Seat
Arthur's Seat is a massive hill with cliffs overlooking Edinburgh. In the spring, beautiful yellow flowers blossom and people flock to take pictures of Edinburgh from above. The difficulty of climbing Arthur's seat is intermediate; although it is not suitable for those with disabilities, most people can reach the top of Arthur's Seat without any particular skills needed. Although it is more difficult to climb when dark, the view at night is also spectacular.
  • Arthur's Seat is a sleeping dragon in Celtic legend. The dragon terrorized the region and consumed livestock until it was full and lied down to nap.
  • Seventeen miniature coffins were once found in a cave on the crags of Arthur's Seat. It is unclear why the coffins were placed there, but rumor has it they are connected to a string of murders committed in the 1800s.
  • The hill was once part of a volcano that is now extinct.
